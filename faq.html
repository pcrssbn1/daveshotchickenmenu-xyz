Frequently Asked Questions About Dave's Hot Chicken Menu

Dave's Hot Chicken has become one of the fastest-growing restaurant chains in America, and with that growth comes plenty of questions from first-time visitors and regular customers alike. This FAQ section addresses the most common inquiries about menu items, pricing, spice levels, nutritional information, and ordering options.

The restaurant's Nashville-style hot chicken concept is straightforward, but the customization options and spice level system can be confusing for newcomers. Below you'll find detailed answers to help you make the most of your Dave's Hot Chicken experience, whether you're dining in, taking out, or ordering delivery.

What items are on Dave's Hot Chicken menu?

Dave's Hot Chicken menu centers on Nashville-style hot chicken prepared as tenders or sliders. Tenders are available in 2, 3, 5, or 8-piece servings, while sliders come individually or in multi-packs. The signature Dave's Sandwich features a chicken breast fillet on a bun with pickles and Dave's sauce. Sides include creamy mac and cheese (a customer favorite), crispy french fries, tangy kale slaw, and cheese fries loaded with melted cheddar. Drinks include soft drinks, lemonade, and bottled water. Some locations offer seasonal desserts like brownies or cookies. Every chicken item can be ordered at any of the seven spice levels, and combo meals pair your chicken choice with a side and drink for better value.

How spicy are the Dave's Hot Chicken heat levels?

Dave's Hot Chicken offers seven distinct heat levels that span from zero spice to extreme pain. No Spice and Lite Mild (100-500 Scoville units) provide flavor without heat. Mild (1,000-2,500 units) offers gentle warmth similar to a pepperoncini. Medium (5,000-8,000 units) delivers noticeable kick comparable to jalapeño peppers. Hot (15,000-20,000 units) brings significant heat that most people find challenging. Extra Hot (50,000-80,000 units) creates intense burning that lingers. The Reaper (500,000+ units) uses Carolina Reaper peppers and requires a waiver at most locations due to extreme capsaicin content. First-timers should start with Mild or Medium and work up gradually. The jump between each level is substantial, not incremental. Ranch dressing and white bread (included with orders) help cool the burn by binding with capsaicin molecules.

Does Dave's Hot Chicken have vegetarian options?

Dave's Hot Chicken offers limited vegetarian options since their specialty is chicken. Vegetarian sides include mac and cheese made with real cheddar, french fries, cheese fries, and kale slaw. The kale slaw contains a creamy dressing that may include dairy, so ask for it on the side if you're vegan. All fried items (fries, cheese fries) are cooked in shared fryers with chicken, so cross-contamination occurs. This makes them unsuitable for strict vegetarians concerned about animal product contact. There are currently no plant-based chicken alternatives like Impossible or Beyond products on the menu. The restaurant focuses exclusively on their core competency: chicken. For vegetarians, a meal might consist of mac and cheese, fries, and kale slaw, which provides decent variety but limited protein options beyond the cheese.

What are Dave's Hot Chicken prices?

Dave's Hot Chicken maintains competitive fast-casual pricing with some regional variation. Sliders cost $3.49-4.49 individually, or $9.99-11.99 for a 3-pack. Chicken tenders run $6.99-7.99 for 2 pieces, $9.49-10.49 for 3 pieces, $14.99-16.49 for 5 pieces, and $19.99-22.99 for 8 pieces. Dave's Sandwich ranges from $10.49-11.99. Combo meals (which include a side and drink) add approximately $4-5 to base prices. Sides are priced at $3.49-4.49 for fries, $4.49-5.49 for mac and cheese, $3.99-4.99 for kale slaw, and $5.49-6.49 for cheese fries. Drinks cost $2.49-3.49. Prices are lowest in markets like Houston and suburban locations, while urban areas like Las Vegas Strip locations charge 15-20% premiums. Third-party delivery adds $3-8 plus service fees. These prices reflect 2024 rates and may increase with inflation.

Can I customize my order at Dave's Hot Chicken?

Yes, customization is central to the Dave's Hot Chicken experience. Every chicken item can be ordered at any of the seven spice levels, from No Spice to The Reaper. You can mix spice levels within an order, so your 3-piece tender meal could include one Mild, one Medium, and one Hot tender. Combo meals let you choose your preferred side from the available options. You can request extra pickles, extra Dave's sauce, or sauce on the side for sandwiches and sliders. Some locations accommodate requests for no pickles or no sauce. You can add sides to any order, and many customers order extra ranch dressing for $0.50-1.00. However, the core menu items themselves don't change—you can't substitute grilled chicken for fried, add vegetables to sandwiches, or request different breading. The customization focuses on spice level and combo components rather than ingredient modifications.

How many calories are in Dave's Hot Chicken menu items?

Calorie counts at Dave's Hot Chicken vary significantly by item and spice level. A single chicken tender contains 160-290 calories depending on size and spice (higher spice levels use more oil-based seasoning). Sliders range from 380-520 calories each, while Dave's Sandwich contains 650-890 calories. A 3-piece tender meal totals 480-870 calories before sides. Mac and cheese adds 420 calories, french fries contribute 380 calories, kale slaw provides 180 calories, and cheese fries contain 520 calories. A complete combo meal with 3 tenders, mac and cheese, and a regular soda can exceed 1,400 calories. The fried preparation method and seasoning oils make these items calorie-dense. For comparison, the USDA recommends 2,000-2,500 daily calories for most adults, so a single combo meal represents 50-70% of that target. Nutritional information is available at most locations and on their website for those tracking intake.

What allergens are in Dave's Hot Chicken food?

Dave's Hot Chicken menu items contain multiple common allergens. The chicken breading contains wheat (gluten), eggs, and milk derivatives. All fried items are cooked in oil that may contain soy. The mac and cheese contains dairy (milk, cheese) and wheat (pasta). Dave's sauce and ranch dressing contain eggs and dairy. Kale slaw dressing typically contains eggs (mayonnaise-based). The restaurant uses shared preparation surfaces and fryers, so cross-contamination between allergens is unavoidable. This makes the restaurant unsuitable for people with celiac disease or severe allergies to wheat, dairy, eggs, or soy. There are no gluten-free breading options, no dairy-free cheese alternatives, and no separate fryers for allergen-free cooking. The kale slaw vegetables themselves are allergen-free, but the dressing contains dairy and eggs. Customers with severe allergies should exercise extreme caution or avoid the restaurant entirely. Detailed allergen information should be requested from individual locations, as recipes may vary slightly.

Does Dave's Hot Chicken offer delivery and online ordering?

Dave's Hot Chicken partners with major third-party delivery services including DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, and Postmates depending on location. Delivery availability varies by market, but most urban and suburban locations offer delivery through at least two platforms. Delivery pricing includes menu markups (typically 15-30% higher than in-store), delivery fees ($3-8), service fees, and tips, which can add $10-15 to your order total. Many locations also offer online ordering for pickup through the Dave's Hot Chicken website or app, which avoids third-party markups. Pickup orders typically take 15-20 minutes during normal times, longer during peak hours. Some locations use their own delivery drivers for nearby orders. The food travels reasonably well, though the chicken loses some crispiness during transport. Ordering Extra Hot or The Reaper for delivery is common since the heat level remains consistent even after cooling. Most delivery platforms allow spice level customization, side selection, and special instructions in the order notes.
